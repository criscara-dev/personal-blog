The common thread across all of it: I like going deep into how things work, then making that complexity useful for someone else. Whether that's building something from scratch, automating a workflow for an internal team, or helping a client actually get value out of the software they're paying for. I can wear different hats; always a client enabler a problem solver and builder.

I write here about tech, what I'm building, and how I think about things. I may also write about my other practices and passions, like movement and art, especially because I keep finding the same ideas turning up across all three. There's more overlap between physical practice, making things, and building software than most people assume.
